Ingredients

  • Miso
  • Doubanjiang (spicy or non-spicy bean paste)
  • Sesame seeds and sesame oil
  • Homemade or store-bought chicken broth
  • White pepper powder

Steps to making Ramen

  1. Select any type of miso for the ramen, but avoid using hatcho miso or saikyo miso.
  2. Use doubanjiang (spicy bean paste) as a key condiment for depth and character; do not substitute if possible.
  3. Start with one teaspoon of doubanjiang for a good kick, adding more if you desire more spiciness.
  4. Consider using non-spicy doubanjiang (often Taiwanese brands like Kangshan or Ming Teh found in Chinese grocery stores) if you want the savory depth without heat.
  5. If you cannot find doubanjiang, you may substitute gochujang (spicy Korean chili paste) or doenjang (non-spicy Korean soybean paste), but be aware the flavor profile will be different.
  6. Grind toasted sesame seeds using a mortar and pestle (suribachi and surikogi), or alternatively use a food processor or coffee grinder, to make the broth nuttier and richer.
  7. Use dark roasted sesame oil (about 1 tablespoon) for its deep flavor and aroma.
  8. Opt for homemade chicken stock for the richest, most flavorful broth, though store-bought broth is acceptable.
  9. When using store-bought broth, choose a less-sodium variety.
  10. Taste the soup before adding any extra salt, as the saltiness of chicken stock can vary significantly.
  11. Add a few sprinkles of white pepper powder to elevate the soup's flavor and provide a kick without intense spiciness.
